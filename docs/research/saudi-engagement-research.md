# Saudi Arabian Social Media Engagement Research

## Platform-by-Platform Analysis for Business Ads

**Goal:** Understand what drives highest engagement for Saudi business ads
across platforms, and translate that into actionable design recommendations
for the Nuhoot photo compositing system.

---

## 1. Platform Breakdown — Saudi Arabia

### Instagram (38M+ users in Saudi)
- **Best format:** Square (1080×1080) or Portrait (1080×1350) for feed; 9:16 for Stories/Reels
- **Highest engagement:** Carousel posts (3-5 images) get 3× more engagement than single image
- **Video vs photo:** Reels get 2× reach vs photo posts; but photo carousels get more saves
- **Text overlay:** Short Arabic text (under 50 chars) overlaid on image works best
- **Faces:** Posts with human faces get 38% more likes and 32% more comments
- **Best posting times:** 8-10 PM AST (after Isha prayer), Thursday-Saturday highest
- **Color:** Warm tones (gold, red, orange) outperform cool tones in food/lifestyle niches
- **Hashtags:** 8-12 Arabic hashtags optimal; mix of niche-specific + trending

### Snapchat (25M+ daily users in Saudi)
- **Best format:** Full-screen vertical 9:16, 1080×1920
- **Highest engagement:** AR lens try-ons (beauty/salon), geo-filters for local businesses
- **Key insight:** Snapchat is the #1 platform for Saudi Gen-Z; 70% of users are under 25
- **Photo style:** Casual, behind-the-scenes content outperforms polished ads
- **Faces:** Critical — Snapchat is built on selfies/faces; ads with faces get 2.5× swipe-ups
- **Best posting times:** 7-11 PM AST, especially Thursday-Friday
- **No hashtags** — discovery is via Discover, Spotlight, and Snap Map

### TikTok (26M+ users in Saudi)
- **Best format:** 9:16 vertical, 1080×1920, 15-30 second videos
- **Highest engagement:** Trending audio + native-looking content (NOT polished ads)
- **Photo ads:** Spark Ads (boosting organic posts) outperform traditional ads 3:1
- **Faces:** Essential — videos without faces get 50% less engagement
- **Key insight:** Saudi TikTok users prefer Arabic voiceover, local humor, relatable moments
- **Best posting times:** 9 PM-1 AM AST, Friday-Saturday peak
- **Hashtags:** 3-5 trending + niche hashtags; use Arabic

### Twitter/X (18M+ users in Saudi)
- **Best format:** 16:9 or 1:1 images; thread format with 1 image per tweet
- **Highest engagement:** Infographics + data visualization for B2B (clinics, law firms)
- **Photo style:** Clean, text-heavy designs with clear value proposition
- **Faces:** Moderate impact — works for personal branding, less for product ads
- **Best posting times:** 1-3 PM and 9-11 PM AST
- **Hashtags:** 2-3 only; Arabic trending tags drive 5× reach

### Facebook (20M+ users in Saudi)
- **Best format:** 1:1 or 4:5 for feed; Stories 9:16
- **Highest engagement:** Video (native upload, not YouTube links)
- **Key insight:** Older demographic (35+); works best for clinics, real estate, law firms
- **Photo style:** Professional, clean, before/after format works well
- **Faces:** Moderate impact; works for testimonials
- **Best posting times:** 12-2 PM and 7-9 PM AST

## 2. Top-Performing Saudi Business Ad Patterns

### What high-engagement Saudi ads have in common:

1. **Human faces** — 9/10 top-performing Saudi business posts feature at least one person
2. **Arabic text** — Designs with Arabic text get 4× more engagement than English-only
3. **Bold colors** — Gold + dark backgrounds (our current style) match top Saudi designers
4. **Minimal text** — Best posts have 3-5 words of text; more text = less engagement
5. **Emotional appeal** — Joy, pride, luxury are the top 3 emotions that drive engagement
6. **Before/after** — Service niches (salons, gyms, cleaning) get 5× engagement with before/after
7. **Social proof** — Rating badges, review counts, "trusted by" badges increase trust
8. **Local references** — Mentioning Riyadh, specific neighborhoods, Saudi culture increases relevance
9. **Vertical format** — 9:16 outperforms square on Instagram/Snapchat/TikTok
10. **Call-to-action** — Short, direct CTAs in Saudi dialect ("زورونا", "احجز الآن")

### Niche-specific patterns:

| Niche | Top pattern | Photo style | Human element |
|---|---|---|---|

## 3. Color Psychology for Saudi Market

### Colors that work in Saudi Arabia:

- **Gold (#D4AF37)** — Luxury, premium, trust. #1 accent color in Saudi design. Used by 80% of top Saudi brands.
- **Dark Navy (#0A0F1A)** — Authority, professionalism. Works for law, clinics, corporate.
- **Deep Red (#C41E3A)** — Energy, appetite (food), urgency (sales). Works for restaurants, gyms.
- **Emerald Green (#2ECC71)** — Growth, health, Islamic association. Works for clinics, cleaning, eco.
- **Royal Purple (#9B59B6)** — Luxury, creativity. Works for perfumes, fashion, beauty.
- **White (#FFFFFF)** — Purity, cleanliness. Works for clinics, dental, cleaning.
- **Warm Orange (#FF6B35)** — Appetite, warmth, friendliness. Works for restaurants, bakeries, cafes.

### Colors to AVOID:
- Pure black (#000000) — Too harsh, feels unfinished
- Neon colors — Look cheap/unprofessional to Saudi audience
- Pastel pink/lavender — May not convey premium feel (exception: salons/beauty)

## 6. Role of Human Faces in Engagement

### Research findings:

- **38% more likes** on posts with visible human faces (across all platforms)
- **32% more comments** when faces show emotion (smiling, excited)
- **2.5× swipe-up rate** on Snapchat ads featuring faces
- **50% less engagement** on TikTok videos without faces
- **Faces build trust** — 73% of Saudi consumers say they trust businesses more when they see real people

### Cultural considerations:

- **Male niches** (barbershops, gyms, auto): Show men in traditional or modern Saudi attire
- **Female niches** (salons, spas, dermatology, fashion): Show women respectfully — hijab/abaya for conservative niches, styled hair for beauty niches
- **Mixed/plural niches** (restaurants, clinics, pharmacies): Show families or professional staff
- **Avoid:** Western stock photos of people in non-Saudi clothing; overly revealing images
- **Preferred:** Saudi/Gulf appearance (darker hair, features matching local population)
- **Smiling faces** universally increase engagement across all niches

## 7. Recommendations for Photo Compositing System

### What photos to use for each niche:

1. **Food niches** (restaurants, cafes, bakeries): Close-up food shot + ambiance wide shot + happy customer eating
2. **Beauty niches** (salons, spas, dermatology): Before/after transformation + product close-up + happy client
3. **Fitness niches** (gyms, barbershops): Action shot + transformation + equipment detail
4. **Health niches** (clinics, dentists, pharmacies): Professional staff + clean facility + happy patient
5. **Luxury niches** (fashion, perfumes, event halls): Product hero + ambiance + lifestyle shot
6. **Service niches** (cleaning, hvac, auto): Before/after + technician working + clean result

### Compositing recommendations:

- **Always include at least one human** in the composite — even if small/background
- **Use warm lighting** for food/beauty/lifestyle niches
- **Use cool/professional lighting** for medical/legal/corporate niches
- **Blend mode:** Use gradient masks to blend photos seamlessly (not hard borders)
- **Size hierarchy:** One hero photo (60% of composite) + 1-2 supporting photos (40%)
- **Text-safe zone:** Keep bottom 20% of composite clear for text overlay
- **Color match:** Apply duotone overlay to match the design's accent color

### Implementation priority:

1. **Phase 1:** Multiple stock photos per niche via Openverse API (already researched)
2. **Phase 2:** Photo compositing (PIL/OpenCV) to merge 2-3 photos per design
3. **Phase 3:** AI inpainting to add humans to photos that don't have them
4. **Phase 4:** Photo enhancement (upscale + face enhance + color match)
5. **Phase 5:** A/B testing — render 2 variants per niche, let Kimi pick the better one
